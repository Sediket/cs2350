Image Formats JPG/GIF/PNG

JPG (Joint Photographic Experts Group)

JPG is a very popular standard for images in use on the internet. The standard was created years ago when size was the primary concern for images, due to this concern JPG images are compressed. Due to this compression JPG images are not ideal for image editing as image quality is lost from the compression. Additionaly, fine lines are distorted, so this format is not ideal for maps either and image quality can become more and more degraded with each save in the JPG format.

GIF (Graphics Interchange Format)

GIF is an older format that is limited to 8-bit and 256 colors and can be animated with each fram also being of 256 colors. Additionaly, GIF images support transparency. Image quality is understandable lower due to its smaller size. GIF unlike JPG is better suited for fine lines and can be used for maps because it is not compressed and doesn't distort the lines. GIF is not ideal for image editing or storage due to not being compressed and low quality. GIFs are primarily used for low resolution animations.

PNG (Portable Network Graphics)

PNG is a new standard that builds on the technologies used by JPG and GIF. For example, PNG supports better transparency than GIFs. PNG supports 8-bit color like GIF, but also supports 24-bit color RGB. PNG files can be compressed without the loss of fine detail unlike JPG. Due to these improvements PNG images are usually larger and might not be supported by very old browsers.
